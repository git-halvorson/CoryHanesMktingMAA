The Problem

The Same Business Can Support Two Very Different Valuations — Depending on the Quality of Its Books

When buyers engage their financial advisors in diligence, those advisors are not simply verifying the numbers you've provided. They are assessing whether your records meet the standard required to support the valuation in your CIM.

Business With Clean Financials
  • CPA-reviewed statements for 3+ years
  • Normalized EBITDA schedule with documented add-backs
  • Consistent accounting treatment year over year
  • Working capital analysis ready for negotiation
  • Clean close process not dependent on the owner
Business Without Clean Financials
  • Internally prepared statements, inconsistently maintained
  • Add-backs embedded in the P&L without documentation
  • Year-over-year variances without explanation
  • Working capital undefined — sets up for a buyer-favorable peg
  • Month-end close requires owner involvement to interpret

The first business moves through diligence cleanly. The second spends weeks answering questions — and often loses ground on valuation in the process. The difference is preparation, not performance.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked

My accountant handles the books. Do I still need to worry about this?

Yes. Many accountants prepare books for tax purposes, not transaction purposes. The standard for a sale process — CPA-reviewed statements, normalized EBITDA schedules, working capital analysis — is different from what most small business accountants produce by default. The scorecard will tell you where the gap is.

Is this really free?

Yes. No credit card, no trial, no catch. We'd rather demonstrate our expertise with something useful than pitch you before you're ready.

What if I'm not planning to sell for a few years?

That's exactly who this is for. Financial record-keeping issues — inconsistent statements, undocumented add-backs, disorganized books — take time to correct. Owners who identify and address these gaps early get significantly better outcomes than those who discover them in the middle of a diligence process.
