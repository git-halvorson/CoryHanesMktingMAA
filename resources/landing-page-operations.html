The Problem

Buyers Are Not Just Acquiring Your Revenue. They Are Acquiring a Business They Can Run Without You.

When a private equity firm or strategic acquirer evaluates your business, they are underwriting a specific risk: will performance be maintained after ownership transfers? If the answer is uncertain, the price reflects that uncertainty.

The question buyers are asking in every management meeting, every operational diligence call, and every reference check is some version of the same thing:

"Does this business run because of the systems, the team, and the processes — or does it run because of this one person?"

If the honest answer is the latter, buyers will protect themselves. Earnouts. Equity rollovers. Consulting requirements. Lower headline price. All of these are mechanisms to transfer transition risk back to the seller.

The owners who command the highest multiples and the cleanest deal structures are the ones who have made themselves genuinely replaceable — before they ever talk to a buyer. This scorecard shows you where you stand on that spectrum.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked

I've built this business from scratch. Won't buyers expect some transition support from me?

Reasonable transition support — 3 to 6 months of consulting — is standard and expected. What buyers are protecting against is a business that cannot function without you permanently. The scorecard helps you distinguish between normal transition involvement and structural dependency that affects valuation.

Is this really free?

Yes. No credit card, no trial, no catch. We'd rather demonstrate our expertise with something useful than pitch you before you're ready.

What if I'm still a few years out from selling?

That's the right time to do this. Reducing owner dependency is one of the most time-intensive parts of exit preparation — it typically takes 18 to 36 months to make meaningful, credible progress. Identifying the gaps now gives you the runway to address them.
