Most Owners Don't Know What Buyers Are Actually Looking For

The moment a serious buyer — a private equity firm, a strategic acquirer, a family office — starts evaluating your business, they're not just reading your P&L.

They're asking:

  • Is this business defensible, or could a competitor eat their lunch in 18 months?
  • Is growth real and sustainable, or is it one big customer and some luck?
  • Are there expansion opportunities the current owner hasn't captured yet?

If you can't answer those questions clearly — with documentation to back it up — buyers discount your price or walk away.

The problem isn't that your business isn't good. It's that your growth story isn't packaged the way buyers need to see it.
