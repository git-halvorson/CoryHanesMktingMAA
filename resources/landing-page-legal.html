The Problem

The Most Expensive Legal Issues Are the Ones Discovered at the Wrong Time

Legal problems that surface during diligence — after a letter of intent is signed and a buyer's advisors have engaged — put sellers in the weakest possible negotiating position. The buyer knows the issue. You know they know. And now they're deciding what it's worth to them to proceed.

The following legal issues are among the most commonly discovered in lower middle market diligence — and the most consistently used to reprice or retrade deals:

Issue Discovered When Typical Impact
IP held in owner's personal name IP diligence, week 2–3 Price hold or closing condition until assigned
Anti-assignment clauses in key contracts Contract review, week 2–4 Closing delay; customer consent required pre-close
Undisclosed minority ownership or equity grants Cap table review, week 1 Deal structure renegotiation; potential LOI retrade
Licenses held by individual, not entity Regulatory review, week 3–5 Re-application required; closing delay of 30–90 days
Undisclosed pending litigation or disputes Legal rep review, week 2–3 Indemnification escrow; potential deal termination

Every one of these is addressable — if you find it first, on your own timeline, before a buyer uses it as leverage.

Frequently Asked

I have an attorney. Shouldn't they have caught these issues already?

General business attorneys are not typically transaction attorneys, and most are not proactively reviewing your business for sale readiness. Transaction counsel engaged at the time of a deal is focused on closing — not on remediation. This scorecard helps you identify the issues worth bringing to your attorney's attention now, before a transaction timeline creates pressure.
