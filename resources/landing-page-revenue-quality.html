Two Businesses. Same Revenue. Very Different Prices.

Imagine two companies, each doing $8M a year.

Business A — Commands a Premium

60% of revenue under multi-year contracts

Renewals running at 90%

Top 3 customers at 25% of revenue

Revenue growing 15% year over year

Business B — Gets Discounted

No formal contracts in place

To a buyer — especially a PE firm running an institutional process — those are not the same business. Not even close. Business A sells for a higher multiple, attracts more competitive offers, and closes faster. Business B either sells at a discount or doesn't sell at all.

The difference isn't the top-line number. It's revenue quality. Most owners don't know how their revenue looks through a buyer's lens. This scorecard shows you.

Common Questions
