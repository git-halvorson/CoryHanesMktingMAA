Section 4 — Growth Story
Scoring Key

Yellow — Needs attention  (1 pt)

1
Competitive Moat
Green  / 2 pts Yellow  / 1 pt Red  / 0 pts
Clear, documented differentiation exists. Customers face material switching costs. The competitive position can be articulated and substantiated independently of the owner. Competitive advantages exist but are not documented. The business is defensible in conversation but relies on owner knowledge to explain — which does not survive a change of ownership. No clear differentiation. The business competes primarily on price, relationships, or geography — advantages that are difficult to transfer and that buyers will discount accordingly.
2
Pipeline Visibility
Green  / 2 pts Yellow  / 1 pt Red  / 0 pts
Pipeline is formally tracked in a CRM or documented system with 12+ months of forward visibility. Management of the pipeline is not dependent on the owner and can be handed off to a buyer. Pipeline exists but is tracked informally — in spreadsheets or the owner's memory. Visibility is inconsistent and the pipeline would be difficult for a buyer to verify or assume control of. No formal pipeline exists. Revenue is largely reactive. Forward visibility is limited to the owner's judgment, which introduces material risk for any prospective buyer.
3
Recent Wins
Green  / 2 pts Yellow  / 1 pt Red  / 0 pts
Three or more notable wins in the past 18 months — new customer logos, contract expansions, new market entry, or product launches — are documented and support a clear forward growth narrative. Wins exist but are not documented or readily articulated. A buyer would need to take the owner's word for the growth trajectory, which introduces diligence friction. No significant new wins in the past 18 months. The business is maintaining existing relationships rather than growing. A buyer must construct the growth thesis independently.
4
Expansion Opportunities
Green  / 2 pts Yellow  / 1 pt Red  / 0 pts
Two or more credible, documented expansion opportunities exist — adjacent markets, new product lines, or geographic entry — with a substantive basis that survives buyer scrutiny in diligence. Expansion ideas are identified but undocumented or speculative. Buyers are unlikely to pay a premium for opportunities that have not been validated or scoped in any material way. No identifiable expansion opportunities. The business has reached steady state, and the burden falls on a buyer to generate upside. This limits the pool of qualified acquirers and constrains valuation.
5
Market Position
Green  / 2 pts Yellow  / 1 pt Red  / 0 pts
The business operates in a growing market and is gaining or holding share. Market tailwinds are documentable and provide a credible foundation for buyer underwriting assumptions. Market position is stable. Neither material growth nor decline is evident. The business is defensible but does not benefit from structural tailwinds that would elevate buyer interest or pricing. Market is declining or the business is losing share to competitors. Buyers will price in secular risk and will require a compelling counter-narrative to underwrite a premium valuation.
